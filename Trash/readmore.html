History of Cabuyao City
As early as the 16th century, the area was already inhabited by Tagalog people. In the 17th century, the town of Cabuyao was established as a visita, or a mission station, of the nearby town of Bay. During the Spanish colonial period, Cabuyao became a center for trade and commerce, with its strategic location along the important trade routes leading to Manila and the southern provinces. In the late 19th century, Cabuyao became a municipality and continued to grow and develop, with agriculture and fishing becoming important industries. During World War II, Cabuyao was occupied by Japanese forces, and many residents suffered from the brutality of the occupation. After the war, Cabuyao continued to thrive, with the establishment of new industries such as manufacturing and electronics. In 1997, Cabuyao was declared a city, becoming the 4th city in Laguna province.
Location and Accessibility
Cabuyao City is situated in the heart of Laguna, making it easily accessible from major cities such as Manila, Quezon City, and Makati. The city is just a few hours’ drive from the Ninoy Aquino International Airport, making it a convenient location for both domestic and international travelers.
